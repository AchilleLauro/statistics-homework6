Fundamental Theorem of Calculus and Its Relationship with PDFs and CDFs

The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (FTC) establishes a crucial link between differentiation and integration. It consists of two parts:

  • First Part: If F(x) is an antiderivative of f(x), then: 
    \( \frac{d}{dx} \int_a^x f(t) \, dt = f(x) \)
  • Second Part: If f(x) is continuous on [a, b], then: 
    \( \int_a^b f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a) \)

Application in Probability Theory

In probability, the FTC explains the relationship between the Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF) and the Probability Density Function (PDF) of a continuous random variable.

Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF)

The CDF, denoted as F(x), represents the probability that a random variable X takes a value less than or equal to x:

\( F(x) = P(X \leq x) \)

It is defined as the integral of the PDF f(t):

\( F(x) = \int_{-\infty}^x f(t) \, dt \)

Probability Density Function (PDF)

The PDF, denoted as f(x), is obtained by differentiating the CDF:

\( f(x) = \frac{d}{dx} F(x) \)

Examples

Example 1: Standard Normal Distribution

The PDF of a standard normal distribution is:

\( f(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{x^2}{2}} \)

The corresponding CDF is:

\( F(x) = \int_{-\infty}^x \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{t^2}{2}} \, dt \)

Differentiating the CDF gives the PDF:

\( \frac{d}{dx} F(x) = f(x) \)

Example 2: Exponential Distribution

For an exponential random variable with rate parameter \( \lambda \), the PDF is:

\( f(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x}, \quad x \geq 0 \)

The CDF is obtained by integrating the PDF:

\( F(x) = \int_0^x \lambda e^{-\lambda t} \, dt = 1 - e^{-\lambda x} \)

Differentiating the CDF gives back the PDF:

\( \frac{d}{dx} F(x) = f(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x} \)

Key Insights and Applications

  • Probabilities Over Intervals: Using the CDF, the probability that a random variable lies in an interval [a, b] is:
    • \( P(a \leq X \leq b) = F(b) - F(a) \)
  • Statistical Inference: Many statistical methods rely on the PDF-CDF relationship, such as maximum likelihood estimation and hypothesis testing.
  • Quantiles and Percentiles: The CDF is crucial for determining quantiles and percentiles, used in descriptive statistics and data analysis.
